This walkthrough is designed to guide a clinician or maker through an adaptive gaming trial. It follows the core phases of preparation, real-time troubleshooting, and post-session documentation.

---

## 1. Before the Gaming Session (Pre-Session)
Success is determined by the planning done before the gamer arrives.

???+ info "Initial Information & Goal Setting"
    **Question:** Has a clear "Gamer Goal" been established?
    
    ??? "If Yes"
        Ensure the session focuses on this specific outcome (e.g., "Playing FIFA with a friend" or "Using a switch to jump in Mario"). If no goal exists, start with a "Low Input" genre to build confidence.

???+ info "Choose Gaming Platform"
    **Question:** Have you verified the compatibility of the chosen platform?
    
    ??? "If Yes"
        Confirm you have the necessary hardware for that system:
        * **PC:** High compatibility for almost all USB/Bluetooth devices.
        * **Xbox:** Best for XAC integration; check if "Copilot Mode" is needed.
        * **PlayStation:** Check if an adapter (Titan Two, etc.) is required for non-DualSense controllers.
        * **Switch:** Ensure you have a USB-to-USB adapter if using an XAC.

???+ info "Choose Trial Game(s)"
    **Question:** Does the selected game offer a low-stress entry point?
    
    ??? "If Yes"
        Look for games with:
        * **Training/Tutorial modes** or "Freeplay."
        * **No fail states** (Creative modes).
        * **Simple menus** that don't require complex navigation to start.

???+ info "Choose Type of Access"
    **Question:** Based on the physical assessment, have you selected the primary input method?
    
    ??? "If Yes"
        Prepare the hardware candidates:
        * **Standard/Modded Controllers:** For those with some hand function.
        * **Mouth Joysticks:** LipSync or Quadstick for head/neck control.
        * **Switch Access:** Positioning external buttons for head, feet, or elbow activation.

???+ info "Additional Hardware & Mounting"
    **Question:** Is the setup stable and positioned correctly for the user?
    
    ??? "If Yes"
        Ensure you have **RAM Mounts**, **Hook and Loop (Velcro)**, or **Lap Trays** ready. Stability is key—if the controller moves, the user loses access.

---

## 2. During the Gaming Session (The Trial)
This phase is about observation and making real-time "on-the-fly" adjustments.

???+ info "Try Initial Setup"
    **Question:** Can the user navigate the game's start screen?
    
    ??? "If Yes"
        Proceed to gameplay. If they struggle with the menu, determine if they need a "digital literacy" explanation or if the button placement is physically unreachable.

???+ info "Identify Barriers and Difficulties"
    **Question:** Are there specific in-game actions that are causing failure?
    
    ??? "If Yes"
        Address the barrier:
        * **Physical Barrier:** Change switch placement or joystick tension.
        * **In-Game Barrier:** Check settings for "Auto-Aim," "Toggle instead of Hold," or "Camera Assist."
        * **Cognitive Barrier:** Simplify the game mode or provide verbal prompts.

???+ info "Fatigue & Frustration Check"
    **Question:** Is the user showing signs of physical strain or irritation?
    
    ??? "If Yes"
        **Pause the session.** Adjust the activation force or take a break. The first session must prioritize a positive experience over technical perfection.

???+ info "Make Record of Setup"
    **Question:** Have you documented the "Winning" configuration?
    
    ??? "If Yes"
        Take photos of the final setup. Document:
        * **Switch mapping:** Which switch does what?
        * **Physical measurements:** Distances and angles of mounts.
        * **In-game settings:** Sensitivity levels and remapping profiles.

---

## 3. After the Gaming Session (Assess & Document)
The goal is to turn a successful trial into a permanent home solution.

???+ info "Assess the Solution"
    **Question:** Did the setup meet the gamer's initial goals?
    
    ??? "If Yes"
        Finalize the "Gaming Setup Equipment List." List every cable, adapter, 3D-printed part, and commercial device used. If the goal wasn't met, schedule a follow-up with different hardware.

???+ info "Contact MMC for Open-Source AT"
    **Question:** Does the setup require custom-built switches or joysticks?
    
    ??? "If Yes"
        Submit a request through the Makers Making Change website. Attach your photos and notes so the volunteer makers can replicate the exact tension and placement you discovered.

???+ info "Send Information to User"
    **Question:** Has the gamer received their "Blueprint"?
    
    ??? "If Yes"
        Provide the gamer/family with:
        1. The photo of the setup.
        2. A list of required in-game settings.
        3. Links to purchase any commercial parts (adapters, controllers).

---

## 4. Final Tips & Tricks
* **Prioritize the Experience:** If a user is struggling, "Challenge by Choice" allows them to step back to an easier mode without feeling like they failed.
* **Maintenance:** Ensure the user knows how to clean their gear (especially mouth-operated devices) and who to call if a 3D-printed part breaks.
* **Starting Small:** It is better to have 2 buttons that work perfectly than 10 buttons that are frustrating to reach.

## How do I Know What I am Looking for?
There is plenty of assistive technology out there for adaptive gaming. Often times there is an overwhelming amount of options and it requires a combination of tools. This can be intimidating. This set of resources may seem overwhelming, but take the approach of determing what you are **NOT** interested in for yourself or the client you are working with. Put everything else on the "maybe" list and then begin evaluating the simplest option. 

### Where you Should Start

Think about these two key questions:
  1. Can the player partially or totally use a standard controller?
    - If yes, look into the controller modifications or software options.
    - If no, look into alternative access section to create a setup with the function they DO have.
  2. Does the player have any sensory (hearing or vision) disabilities or mental health considerations?
    - If yes, look into the software options.

We want to think about what abilities and areas of access the player DOES have and utilize those as inputs into the game. For example, someone with one hand can use the left side of a Xbox controller totally fine but needs access on the right. Let's first look at the controller modifcaiton options to see if we can get a one handed controller for them. If all those options do not look like a good fit, move into the alternative access to create a custom setup. It is also likley that they may need both, a one handed controller modification that allows them to game with one hand, but also maybe voice control for inputs that the modification does not work great for.

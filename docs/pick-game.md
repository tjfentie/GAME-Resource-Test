## "What Games are Accessible?"
We get this question a lot, and the answer is almost always: **it depends.** Accessibility is highly personal. Whether a game works for you depends on several factors:
* What **genre** of game do you enjoy?
* What **gaming system** (PC, Console, Mobile) are you using?
* Do you want to play **online** with others or solo?
* What specific **accessibility settings** (visual, auditory, cognitive, or motor) do you require?
* How many **physical inputs** (switches, buttons, joysticks) can you reliably use?

Because the landscape changes with every game update, we recommend using dedicated resources to find the specific features you need.

---

## Resources for Finding Accessible Games
Rather than listing every game, we recommend these databases which are maintained by experts and disabled gamers:

* **[CanIPlayThat](https://caniplaythat.com/):** A fantastic resource featuring deep-dive articles and reviews on game accessibility written by disabled gamers themselves.
* **[Family Gaming Database](https://www.taminggaming.com/):** A massive database of board and video games. You can search by specific accessibility features—such as "games that can be played with one switch"—making it a powerful tool for finding exact matches for your setup.
* **[AbleToPlay](https://abletoplay.gg/):** This site allows you to create a personalized profile based on your specific needs. It then generates customized game recommendations and "compatibility scores" to show how well a game might work for you.

## General Guidance for Input Levels
Different genres of games require different levels of physical coordination and button complexity. If you are just starting your gaming journey, beginning with a "Low Input" genre can help reduce frustration while you get used to your assistive technology.

### Input Level Definitions
* **High:** Frequently requires two joysticks and multiple buttons that may need to be held simultaneously or pressed rapidly. High precision and fast reflexes are critical.
* **Medium:** Often requires two joysticks and several buttons, but the gameplay can usually be taken at the player's own pace.
* **Low:** Typically requires only one joystick and a limited number of buttons, or in some cases, buttons only.

### Genre Breakdown

| Genre | Input Level | Description | Examples |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Puzzle** | **Low** | Requires solving logic problems or navigating cognitively geared challenges. Often turn-based with no time pressure. | *Portal, Candy Crush, Hidden Folks, Donut County* |
| **Platformer** | **Low** | Usually 2D movement (left/right) with jumping and basic actions to navigate levels. | *Super Mario Bros, Celeste, Cuphead* |
| **Fighting** | **Low** | Combat between characters, often on a 2D plane. Can be played against friends or the computer. | *Brawlhalla, Street Fighter, Dragon Ball Fighter Z* |
| **Racing** | **Low** | Competitive or free-roam driving. Many offer "auto-drive" or simplified steering assists. | *Mario Kart, Need for Speed, Dirt 5* |
| **Sandbox** | **Medium** | Players enter a world to customize, shape, and create their own path with little to no forced storyline. | *Minecraft, Terraria, Stardew Valley* |
| **Sports** | **Medium** | Simulates professional sports where you manage a team or play as an individual athlete. | *Nintendo Switch Sports, FIFA, NHL* |
| **Action / Adventure** | **High** | Story-driven games featuring exploration and combat. Requires complex camera and character movement. | *Spider-Man, Assassin's Creed, Far Cry* |
| **Shooter / Battle Royale** | **High** | Fast-paced games played from a first-person perspective requiring high precision aiming and quick reactions. | *Call of Duty, Fortnite, Doom* |
| **MOBA** | **High** | Large-scale strategy games where teams work together to defeat the opposition in real-time. | *League of Legends, Dota 2, Smite* |

---

### A Note on Experience
Choosing a game with lower input requirements does **not** mean sacrificing the quality of your experience. There is an incredible variety of art styles, deep storytelling, and immersive worlds within the "Low" and "Medium" input categories. Start where you feel comfortable and expand your library as you master your setup!
